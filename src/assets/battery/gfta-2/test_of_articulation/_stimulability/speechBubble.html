Instructions

Examinee requires repetition
Repetitions are allowed when requested or when it appears that the examinee requires redirection.
Examinee makes an omission or distortion in Sounds-in-Words or Sounds-in-Sentences
Only sounds that were misarticulated during Sounds-in-Words or Sounds-in-Sentences will be assessed in Stimulability. Sounds will be assessed only in the position that the error occurred. 
Examinee requires evaluation during Stimulability at different levels of complexity
You can test stimulability for misarticulated sounds at the syllable level only or you can test at all three levels of complexity (syllable, word, and sentence). If the examinee succeeds in imitating the syllable, go on to word imitation. If the examinee succeeds in imitating at the word, go on to sentence imitation.
Examinee fails to imitate a stimulus correctly at any level (syllable, word, or sentence)
Ask the examinee to watch and listen. Then follow these procedures:
  1. Say the stimulus three times.
  2. Have the examinee try again.
  3. If the examinee imitates successfully after multiple stimulation, continue with the next level of complexity for that sound.
  4. If the examinee cannot repeat the stimulus correctly after multiple stimulation, discontinue stimulability testing for that sound.
