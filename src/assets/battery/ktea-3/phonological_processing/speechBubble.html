Instructions
Examinee appears to not understand sample or teaching items
Teaching is especially important on this subtest. Make every effort to ensure the examinee understands what he or she is being asked to do. Teaching is only permitted where indicated.
Examinee requests a repetition
You may repeat an item at the examinee's request or if you think he or she did not hear it.
SAMPLE C & ITEMS 14–18 (RHYMING)
Examinee says a nonword or real word that ends with the same sound
Award credit if the response rhymes with the target word. Correct responses include ending rhymes that are spelled differently (e.g., stir rhymes with fur).
ITEMS 25–50 (DELETING SOUNDS & SEGMENTING)
Examinee does not repeat back the correct target word (e.g., for Item 25, does not repeat back “cried,” before being instructed to say it again without the /d/ sound)
Re-administer the item. If the examinee does not repeat the correct word after several tries, select the Fails to Repeat (FR) contextual event button and continue administration.
ITEMS 36–50 (SEGMENTING)
Examinee provides a marginally segmented response to a sample or teaching item
For a response to be scored as correct, the examinee must make a noticeable pause between each part of the word. Use the sample and teaching items to teach the examinee to pause long enough between word parts.
Examinee segments a word into three or more syllables (for syllable segmenting items)
Award credit if the response includes the correct number of syllables (and pronunciation); the syllables can be segmented in different ways (e.g., fan...tas...tic and fan...tast...ic are both correct).
