General Instructions
Before administering this subtest for the first time, listen to the audio files for examples of how to present Sections 1 and 4. Audio files are stored in Q-i Central under the Support tab (select Test Materials).

In some sections, you are asked to exaggerate or emphasize particular sounds within words (such as the final consonant). Do this by clearly articulating and slightly elongating the sound; do not make the sound louder.

When a letter is surrounded by forward slashes, say the letter sound rather than the letter name (e.g., /m/ is pronounced mmm, not em).

When administering the Blending and Segmenting sections, the KTEA–II provided the option of using a hand puppet, Pepper. Pepper is not provided in the KTEA–3 kit, but using a puppet is still permitted when administering these sections. If you choose to use a puppet, follow the modified administration instructions included at the beginning of the Blending and Segmenting sections.
 
When administering the Blending section, read target words in parts, with each part separated by ellipses (…); pause 1 second between parts of the word.
 
When administering the Sound Matching section, emphasize the final sound of each stimulus word by either lengthening the final sound (e.g., treeee) or exaggerating it (e.g., hat).

Recording

Don’t Know (DK): Used to record when the examinee provides an answer of “I don’t know” as his or her only answer.

No Response (NR): Used to record when the examinee does not respond.

Repeated Item (R): Used to record when the examinee asks for an item to be repeated or when it appears that he or she was not attending.

Fails to Repeat (FR): Used to record when the examinee does not repeat the presented word after several attempts for a given item in Deleting Sounds or Segmenting.

Scoring

Score 0 or 1 point.

Generally, do not penalize for articulation differences due to dialect or regional speech patterns. For example, l a b e n d e r is an acceptable response for lavender in Section 5, as is suh prise for surprise in Section 1. However, the correct number of sounds must be provided. For example, tes instead of test is not an acceptable response in Section 4.

Do not penalize for exaggerated single phoneme sounds (e.g., guh...oh for g...oh).

Refer to the KTEA–3 Administration and Scoring Manuals for additional information.
