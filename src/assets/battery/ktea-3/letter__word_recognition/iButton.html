Materials

Client Device (Raised)

Administration

Prior to administration, listen to the audio files for Letter & Word Recognition (reference use only). Audio files are stored in Q-i Central under the Support tab (select Test Materials).

Start
Grades PK–K: Item 1
Grade 1: Item 17
Grade 2: Item 22
Grade 3: Item 28
Grades 4–7: Item 40
Grades 8–12+: Item 52

Reverse
Grades 1–12+: Must pass the first 3 items administered, or drop back to the previous section. Repeat as necessary until 3 consecutive items are passed or Item 1 is reached. Allow the examinee to complete remaining items on the client device before reversing.

Discontinue
After 4 consecutive scores of 0. Allow the examinee to complete remaining items on the client device before discontinuing. Credit is not awarded for items beyond the discontinue point.

Recording

Don’t Know (DK): Used to record when the examinee provides an answer of “I don’t know” as his or her only answer.

No Response (NR): Used to record when the examinee does not respond.

Self-Correct (SC): Used to record when the examinee self-corrects.

Scoring

Score 0 or 1 point.

  • Do not attempt to change item raw scores in the Review Mode or the All-To-Do overlay. All scoring must be completed during the administration. 
  • Some words have more than one acceptable pronunciation. The syllable breaks should not be applied rigidly.

  • For credit, the examinee must say the word as a connected, relatively smooth whole, with the accent on the proper syllable. There is no penalty for earlier partial or disconnected pronunciations as long as the final, smooth pronunciation is correct.

  • Do not penalize for articulation differences due to dialect, regional speech patterns, or a first language other than English (e.g., an examinee whose first language is Spanish may pronounce the letter v as /b/).

  • Do not penalize for pronouncing tu as tyoo instead of too or choo (e.g., tumultuous may be pronounced as tyoo MUHL tyoo us).

  • Do not penalize for the American dialectal variation of pronouncing /t/ as /d/ in certain words, such as saying yoo NYE did for united or pen TAM uh dur for pentameter.

  • Items before the basal will be counted as correct regardless of the score entered.

Refer to the KTEA–3 Administration and Scoring Manuals for additional information.
