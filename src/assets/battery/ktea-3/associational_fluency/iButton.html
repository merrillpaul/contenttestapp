Administration

Start
Grades PK–12+: Item 1

Discontinue
Do not discontinue.

Timing

Allow exactly 60 seconds for each item.

Recording

Don’t Know (DK): Used to record when the examinee provides an answer of “I don’t know” as his or her only answer.

No Response (NR): Used to record when the examinee does not respond.

Prompt (P): Used to record when an item-specific prompt is provided.

Scoring

Score 1 point for each valid, unique response.

Count the following as correct:

  • Item 1
    • Anything that is recognizable as a food or beverage, or that is consumed for nourishment (e.g., pizza, pasta, water, dog food, vitamins = 5 points)
    • Foods from different cultures or regions (e.g., Spanish words such as tamal, pan)
    • Names of fast food restaurants
    • Ingredients, spices, and condiments
  • Item 2
    • Anything that is recognizable or conceivable as a color
    • Foods or objects that are usually, mostly one color
    • Award credit for slight variations or synonyms of colors already named (e.g., red, crimson, scarlet, cherry = 4 points)
    • Award credit for shades and variations of colors (e.g., blue-green, green-blue, red-orange, blue, dark blue, light blue = 6 points)

Do not count:

  • Words given as samples in the prompt (Item 1: cookie, salad; Item 2: yellow, beige)
  • Exact repetitions, unless they could be homophones
  • Made-up words
  • Both singular and plural forms of a word
  • More than one of a set of words that bring to mind the same visual image or concept (e.g., accept fries or French fries, but not both; accept soda or pop, but not both). Responses should get credit only when the responses bring to mind different visual images.
  • Meal times such as lunch, dinner, and snack

Refer to the KTEA–3 Administration and Scoring Manuals for additional information.
