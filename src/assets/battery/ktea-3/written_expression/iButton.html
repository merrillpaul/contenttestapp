Materials

Client Device, Levels 2–4 only (Raised)
Appropriate response booklet, as follows:
  • Grades PK–K: Response Booklet, Form A
  • Grades 1–2: Written Expression Booklet, Form A, Level 2
  • Grades 3–5: Written Expression Booklet, Form A, Level 3
  • Grades 6–12+: Written Expression Booklet, Form A, Level 4
Pencil with eraser

Administration

Start
Grades PK–K: Level 1, Items 1–16
Grades 1–2: Level 2, Items 17–37
Grades 3–5: Level 3, Items 38–56
Grades 6–12+: Level 4, Items 57–67
An examinee who is expected to perform well below or above average for his or her grade may be administered a lower or higher level than the one recommended.

Reverse
Levels 2–4: If the first several items seem difficult (e.g., if the examinee becomes too fatigued or discouraged to complete all items in the level, or if an examinee earns a score of 0 on the vast majority of items in the level), consider manually discontinuing administration and administering the next lower level at a later time.

Discontinue
Level 1 only: Manually discontinue if the examinee scores 0 on all of Items 3–10.

General Instructions
Because the purpose of the story format is to engage the examinee in the writing tasks, it is important to emphasize the story when presenting the subtest. Encourage the examinee to attempt all items, so that he or she will be able to do the essay at the end of the booklet (Levels 2–4).

Recording

Don’t Know (DK): Used to record when the examinee provides an answer of “I don’t know” as his or her only answer.

No Response (NR): Used to record when the examinee does not respond.

Repeated Item (R): Used to record when the examinee asks for an item to be repeated or when it appears that he or she was not attending.

Scoring

Non-essay items: Follow the scoring guidelines provided for each criterion and score 0, 1, or 2 points for each criterion within the item.

Essay items (Level 2, Item 37; Level 3, Item 56; Level 4, Item 67): Follow the scoring guidelines provided for each criterion.

Refer to the KTEA–3 Administration and Scoring Manuals for additional information.
