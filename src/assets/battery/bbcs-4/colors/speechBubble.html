• Introduce each test item by saying, Show me. . . After the child fully understands the task, you may omit the Show me. . . part of the item and read just the item (e.g., which animal is big). Be certain to introduce at least the first item of each subtest by saying, Show me. . . to reorient the child to the task.
  • Repeat an item if the child does not respond after about 10 seconds or if the child requests it. Do not repeat an item if the child responds incorrectly after the first presentation unless the child requests it.
  • Begin each subtest by saying, Look at all of the pictures. If, at any point during testing, the child falls into a pattern of pointing to pictures located in only one position on the stimulus page, again say, Look at all of the pictures. If the child continues to point to the same location, present the next item by pointing to each of the four pictures while saying, Look at this picture, and this picture, and this one, and this one. Watch the child’s eyes to make sure he or she is following your directions and is looking at each stimulus option. Then read the item. Continue the cues to look at all the pictures as necessary.
  • If the child points to more than one picture, say, You can only show me or point to one picture. Show me. . ., and record the child’s next response. If the child continues to point to more than one picture for that item, record the multiple responses and score the item as 0.
  • Sometimes a child will “self-correct” (correctly revise his or her incorrect response to a test item). If the self-correction occurs before presentation of the next item, record and score the self-corrected response.
  • If the child responds with the correct response, then changes his or her response to an incorrect response, record the final (incorrect) response and score the item as 0.
