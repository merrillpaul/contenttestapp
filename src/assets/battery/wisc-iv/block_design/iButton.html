Administration

Start
Ages 6-7: Item 1
Ages 8-16: Item 3
Children suspected of intellectual deficiency should start on Item 1.

Reverse
Ages 8-16: Score of 0 or 1 on either of the first two items given, administer preceding items in reverse order until two consecutive perfect scores are obtained. 

Discontinue
After 3 consecutive scores of 0

Timing

Accurate timing is essential, particularly for items awarding bonus points. The time limit for each item is provided in the timer on the Examiner Device. 

Begin timing for each item after saying the last word of the instructions. 

Stop timing for each item when the child completes the item, the time limit expires, or it is clear from the child's words or gestures that he or she has finished. If uncertain, ask the child if he or she has finished working.

Recording

Don't Know (DK): Used to record when the child provides an answer of "I don't know" as his or her only answer

No Response (NR): Used to record when the child does not respond

Prompt Administered (P): Used to record when any subtest-specific prompt is provided 

Gap over ¼" (Gap): Used to record when the child's final design has one or more gaps, bigger than a ¼", between the blocks

Blocks rotated 30° or more (Rotates >30°): Used to record when the child's final design is rotated 30° or more 

Configuration Break (Config. Break): Used to record when the child's final design violates the standard 2 X 1, 2 X 2, or 3 X 3 block configuration  

Scoring

Items 1-3: Score 0, 1, or 2 points.
Items 4-8: Score 0 or 4 points.
Items 9-14: Score 0 or the appropriate time bonus score.

Refer to the WISC-IV Administration and Scoring Manual for additional information.
