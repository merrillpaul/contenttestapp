Instructions

ITEMS 1 - 4

Provide these queries as often as necessary, but do not query responses that are clearly incorrect: 
Marginal response (e.g., “engine” to the train): Say, Yes, but what else is it called?
 
Generalized response (e.g., “plant” to the flower): Say, Yes, but what kind of [insert child’s response] is it?
 
Functional description (e.g., “It’s a water holder” to the bucket): Point to the picture and say, Yes, but what is it called?
 
Hand gesture (e.g., pretending to turn steering wheel in response to the car): Say, Yes, but what is it called?
ITEMS 5 - 36
Child points to an object in the room and does not respond orally 
Say, Tell me in words what a [insert stimulus word] is.
Child hears a different word and responds incorrectly 
Say, Listen carefully, what does [insert stimulus word] mean? or make a similarly neutral inquiry.
Child asks you to repeat an item 
Each word may be repeated as often as necessary, but do not alter the wording in any way.
Child’s response is unclear or followed by a Q in the sample responses
Say, What do you mean? or Tell me more about it.
